Moving to Marseille: first impressions.

A few weeks in, and I'm still figuring out whether Marseille is chaotic or just honest. Either way, I think I like it.

marseille phd life new city
◫ Cover image · ../../photos/blog/blog-1.jpg

The first thing you notice about Marseille is the light. It hits differently here — sharp, flat, relentless even in January. Coming from Bologna, where the fog sits on the city like a lid, it takes some getting used to.

I moved here at the start of the year to begin my PhD at CNRS–LMA. The lab is on the edge of the city, in a quiet corner that feels nothing like the chaos of the Vieux-Port. But the chaos is always just a tram ride away, and I like that.

The city

Marseille is a port city and it shows. There's a roughness to it, an unfinished quality, like it's always in the middle of becoming something. Markets that smell of fish and spices, streets where the architecture doesn't quite match, people who speak too fast and too loud and are somehow always right.

The Calanques are thirty minutes from the city centre by bus. Limestone cliffs dropping straight into the sea, the water so clear it looks edited. I went on a Saturday, and even in winter there were people swimming. I didn't swim. Next time.

The lab

LMA is a good place to work. Quiet, serious, international in a way that French institutions sometimes aren't. My desk has a window. The coffee machine is two floors down and worth the trip.

I'm still finding my feet, research-wise. But the questions are interesting, the people are kind, and the city is impossible to be bored in. Good enough for now.
